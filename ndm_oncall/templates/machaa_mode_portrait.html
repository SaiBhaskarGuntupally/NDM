HOT 10 Questions
If they ask: “We got your profile from our database. Can you confirm interest?”
You say: “Yes, I’m open. Please share the role details and client.”
You ask back: “Was my profile submitted earlier for this role?”
If they ask: “We are checking if you’re already in the system.”
You say: “I’m not sure. Please confirm on your side.”
You ask back: “If it’s a duplicate, can you remove the earlier submission?”
If they ask: “It’s confidential for now. Are you still interested?”
You say: “I’m interested, but I need client context before moving.”
You ask back: “Is the client name available after NDA or submission?”
If they ask: “We are the vendor; partners are involved.”
You say: “Understood. I want to avoid duplicate submissions.”
You ask back: “How many layers are between you and the end client?”
If they ask: “What is your preferred engagement?”
You say: “I’m flexible based on client need.”
You ask back: “Which option is approved for this role?”
If they ask: “What rate are you expecting?”
You say: “I’m open to a fair market rate for this role.”
You ask back: “What is the approved rate range from the client?”
If they ask: “What visa do you have?”
You say: “I’m eligible to work. Let’s align to client requirements.”
You ask back: “Is there a specific visa requirement from the client?”
If they ask: “Can you share your passport now?”
You say: “I can share at the right stage per client policy.”
You ask back: “At what stage is passport required for this client?”
If they ask: “We are collecting profiles. Are you available?”
You say: “Yes, I’m available. I’d like to understand the timeline.”
You ask back: “Are interviews already in progress?”
If they ask: “We will follow up once we hear back.”
You say: “Great, I’ll wait for your update.”
You ask back: “What timeline should I expect for feedback?”

Interview Cheat Sheet

Key Talking Points

  • Start with context: confirm role, team size, current roadmap
  • Ask about biggest pain points or challenges
  • Align candidate experience to team needs
  • Close with hiring timeline and next steps

Questions to Ask

  • What does success look like in the first 30, 60, and 90 days?
  • What are the current technical challenges the team is facing?
  • How does the team collaborate and communicate daily?

Common Objections

  • Concern about scope: Reframe around outcomes and proven delivery cadence. Offer to share decision docs or examples.
  • Technical depth questions: Highlight systems design thinking and measurable impact on past projects.

Key Metrics to Mention

  • Performance improvements delivered
  • Team velocity and ship cadence
  • User satisfaction or retention gains
  • Cross-functional collaboration examples

Closing Notes

  • Confirm next steps clearly. Ask for feedback on fit. Express enthusiasm for the role and team mission.
